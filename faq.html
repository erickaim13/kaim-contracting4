Drainage

Standing water usually means a grading or drainage issue. We diagnose the cause (poor slope, clogged sub-surface, downspout placement) and install solutions like French drains, catch basins, dry wells, or regrading to move water away from the property.

Costs vary by length, depth, and what we have to dig through. A typical residential French drain runs $30 to $80 per linear foot installed. We will give a firm written quote after assessing the site.

Pressure Washing

Pressure washing uses high-pressure water to blast dirt off hard surfaces like concrete driveways, brick patios, and pavers. Soft washing uses low-pressure water combined with biodegradable cleaning solutions to gently clean delicate surfaces like vinyl siding, painted wood, stucco, and asphalt roofs. Using high pressure on siding or a roof can force water behind panels, strip paint, tear roof granules, or damage trim. Most house exteriors should be soft washed, not pressure washed.

Concrete and paver driveways, walkways, patios, decks, siding, fences, and pool surrounds. We adjust pressure and use the right detergents for each surface so nothing gets damaged. We do not pressure wash asphalt or blacktop, since the pressure can damage the surface.

Most homes benefit from a full exterior wash every 1 to 2 years to prevent mold, algae, and dirt buildup. Driveways and walkways do well with annual cleaning, especially after winter.
